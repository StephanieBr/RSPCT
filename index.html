RSPCT connects startups with software developers

Every three months we conduct The RSPCT Event connecting accepted startups and software developers. At RSPCT we set a clear bar of what level of programming ability is expected of interns and software developers and we make sure that the event is productive for everyone involved.

How we do it

If you are a developer, to participate in RSPCT, you must demonstrate your software development skills with code you have posted on GitHub. We also take into consideration past projects, online classes, hackathons, workshops and programming challenges you have completed.

We review each and every applicant and then have a personal conversation to understand what your software development abilities are. If you are qualified we invite you to our next RSPCT event, but if a you are interested but is lacking in some area, we welcome you to our RSPCT learning community where you’ll be in an environment surrounded by peers and experienced programmers all improving their skills and gaining experience.

RSPCT Community

Irrespective of whether you are a startup or a developer, you are more than welcome to our warm online community where people learn and practice developing software with peers and experienced programmers. We also hold regular offline meetups at Jaaga where people come together to discuss and to participate in hands on sessions to reinforce what they are learning.

For learners interested in programming, you’ll go through a designed learning path of your choosing at your own pace. You’ll be surrounded by peers who are going through the same learning process as well as industry experts who can help you along the way. Moreover a lot of interesting and fun individuals hangout in our community. You should check it out!
