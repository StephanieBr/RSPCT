RSPCT connects startups with software developers

We conduct regular events connecting accepted startups and software developers for internships and jobs. At RSPCT we set a clear bar of what level of programming ability is expected out of developers and we make sure that the event is productive for everyone involved.

The next RSPCT is on April 11th

How we do it

If you are a developer, to participate in RSPCT, you must demonstrate your software development skills with code you have posted on GitHub. We also take into consideration past projects, online classes, hackathons, workshops and programming challenges you have completed.

We review your application and will have personal conversations with you to understand your programming abilities. If you are qualified, we invite you to our next RSPCT event, otherwise we give you advice about specific things you can do to improve your skills.

If you are really interested in improving your skills, we invite you to our Learn to Code community where people, beginners and experienced alike, work together in practicing and improving their programming skills.

The RSPCT Learn to Code Community

We believe anyone can become a solid software developer if you have the interest, discipline and is willing to put in the time and regular effort to learn the necessary skills. Writing good software requires clear thinking, practice and a familiarity with a variety of tools. Like medicine, law, or most other serious professions, one should expect to spend years getting good at it.

Fortunately everything one needs to know to become a professional software developer is freely available on the internet. Most software professionals taught themselves most of what they know by working on projects and exploring the internet for solutions to their problems.

We assist people who are serious in pursuing this path. The RSPCT Event we conduct provides a clear target to shoot for. The community guides people as to what they can study, gives support when people have questions, and arranges a variety of activities, both offline and online, to help people stay focused and motivated as they start building web and mobile applications.
